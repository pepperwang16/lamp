Observations and Proposal

User Observations

Student (Library)

  • Checks phone regularly, often around :00 or :30; glances at phone then quickly packs up and leaves.
  • Notices peers carrying umbrellas → checks own phone; asks “Is it raining? Did you bring an umbrella?”
  • Opens timer/calendar app on laptop or tablet; checks phone before leaving/entering the library.
  • Remains seated without stretching or drinking water; hunched forward while studying.

Researcher (Lab)

  • Glances at laptop clock or calendar to gauge elapsed time; briefly unlocks phone to check notifications.
  • Hesitates before starting work; reorganizes notes/tabs, then enters deep focus for long stretches.
  • Hunched posture; frustrated when interrupted and needs time to regain flow; walks out still thinking/making notes.

Worker (Office)

  • Glances at phone and leaves in a rush; reacts to coworkers with umbrellas by checking phone.
  • Opens weather tab; looks out the window; works long periods without moving.
  • Remarks like “Oh no, it’s raining already—I didn’t bring an umbrella”; checks phone at entry/exit; leans forward over keyboard.

Identified Problems

  1. Reliance on phones for time & weather → distractions from unrelated notifications; frequent checks at :00/:30.
  2. Sudden & unprepared departures → rushing out and forgetting items; weather preparedness is reactive.
  3. Weather awareness via indirect/social cues → asking peers / looking outside instead of consistent signals.
  4. Neglect of physical well-being → extended sitting without stretching/hydrating; fatigue builds up.

Opportunity for a Calm, Ambient System

  • Provide glanceable cues for time & weather to reduce phone-checking.
  • Support smooth, prepared transitions between spaces (leave on time, dress for weather).
  • Encourage healthy micro-breaks (stretch/hydrate) via gentle, non-intrusive light signals.

Personas & Scenarios

Persona 1 — The Student (Sarah Kim, 21)

Goals: Deep focus without disruptive alarms; remember to hydrate/stretch; be weather-prepared when leaving.
Frustrations: Loud timers in quiet spaces; forgets breaks; gets caught by sudden weather.
How the Lamp helps: Ambient focus/break cues; gradient color shows upcoming weather; gentle reminders for micro-breaks.

  • Scenario 1a — Study & Breaks: Soft glow shift indicates break time (e.g., 40/10 cycle) without startling peers.
  • Scenario 1b — Weather Awareness: Gradient reflects near-term forecast so she packs an umbrella/jacket in advance.

Persona 2 — The Researcher (Dr. Amir Hassan, 34)

Goals: Maintain concentration; respect quiet lab; avoid fatigue; prepare for meetings/commute with advance weather cues.
Frustrations: Phone alerts break flow; skips breaks; misses weather changes before events.
How the Lamp helps: Silent light cues manage focus/breaks; posture/hydration nudges; color/gradient communicates forecast.

  • Scenario 2a — Focus & Breaks: Gradual light change reminds him to stretch; optional silent meeting alarm.
  • Scenario 2b — Weather Awareness: Subtle palette shift signals rain/temperature changes for better planning.

Persona 3 — The Worker (Alex Chen, 28)

Goals: Leave home/work prepared for rain/snow; monitor weather without distractions; sustain productivity with gentle breaks.
Frustrations: Forgets checks and gets caught by rain; phone apps derail focus; intrusive alerts.
How the Lamp helps: Ambient weather awareness; light-based break nudges; unobtrusive planning for commute/lunch.

  • Scenario 3a — Morning Preparation: Quick read of current + next hours to dress appropriately before rushing out.
  • Scenario 3b — During Workday: Cooler hue signals incoming rain → adjust lunch/leave time to avoid bad weather.

Related Products (Comparative Notes)

Forest (Pomodoro) — effective but phone-centric; screens/numerical countdowns/sounds can raise anxiety and distract in quiet spaces.

Google Nest Hub — requires explicit interaction; screen/voice feedback can be disruptive; information density exceeds calm-tech needs.

Why our Lamp? Removes screens & numbers; relies on gentle ambient transitions, better for libraries/labs and sustained focus.

Feasibility (Summary)

  • Hardware: ESP32/Raspberry Pi + addressable RGB LEDs + diffuser housing.
  • Software: Weather API → color mapping; timer logic on microcontroller; lightweight local web UI.
  • Team: Hardware prototyping, firmware & API integration, UX research and evaluation coverage.
  • Effort: ~22–26 hours per member (assembly, firmware, API, web UI, user testing, documentation).
